# Lore Notes

This file is the living source of truth for the game's worldbuilding.

## Confirmed Ideas

- The game currently has a generic world, a central settlement, and dungeons planned for later.
- The world should feel cozy and mysterious.
- The central settlement is likely a trading post, which can naturally introduce new characters, travelers, rumors, goods, and factions over time.
- The trading post exists because it sits at the center of roads leading north, west, east, and south.
- Three roads openly connect the trading post to three known city-states.
- A fourth road once connected the trading post to a fourth city-state, but that road is now hidden or blocked by a major natural feature such as mountains, snow, jungle, or another barrier.
- The three city-states depend on one another.
- The three city-states will likely have broad roles: food, tools/crafts/machinery, and knowledge/magic/religion/law. These roles need more depth before they are final.

## City-States

### Food City-State

- Role: produces food and feeds travelers.
- It is run by old families with strict traditions.
- It has a sacred or cultural duty as a pilgrim kitchen city, offering food and hospitality to travelers and pilgrims.
- It protects a seed vault containing rare, old, or important seeds.
- Skill affinities: herbalism, alchemy, fishing, cooking, and ranged or agility-based combat styles.

### Repair City-State

- Role: makes, maintains, and repairs tools, crafts, and machinery.
- Its culture believes almost everything can be repaired and almost nothing should be thrown away.
- This should support funny conversations about repairing "everything," including objects that probably should have been replaced long ago.
- Skill affinities: blacksmithing, mining, crafting, leatherworking, and melee combat styles.

### Knowledge-Magic City-State

- Role: preserves and develops knowledge and magic.
- Its people are more sophisticated and high-class than the other city-states.
- Its culture likely values education, refinement, status, and magical expertise.
- Skill affinities: enchanting, restoration, and magic combat styles.

## Trading Post

- The trading post has a shared market connected to all three city-states.
- The private homes and local life of the trading post are independent from the city-states.
- A mayor governs the trading post.
- The mayor is cheerful in public but worried in private.
- The mayor is privately worried about an undefined emerging evil that should be able to unfold into a series of dungeons or raids.
- The trading post acts as the Fourth Little Power: officially much smaller than the city-states, but important because it controls daily crossroads life, local trust, and trade relationships.

## Threat Structure

- The story should have one big overarching evil or threat, but it should not be obvious at first.
- Early dungeons should feel like local problems with smaller forms of evil.
- Later, the player should discover that these local problems are connected to the larger story.
- The structure should have a Defias-like feeling: grounded early conflicts that seem local, practical, or political, but gradually reveal deeper causes and a larger design.
- Current main threat direction: a combination of the False Ledger and the Sleeping Fourth City.
- The hidden faction is connected to a disappeared fourth city-state.
- The fourth city-state is not underneath the trading post. It was one of four directional city-states around the central trading post.
- The fourth city-state likely vanished from common life after its people were erased, exiled, scattered, or stripped of recognition.
- One road from the trading post to the fourth city-state is hidden or blocked by a natural feature.
- This faction wants the disappeared fourth city-state to return to power.
- The fourth city-state and its hidden faction should be morally complicated: dangerous in the present, but with a real grievance, understandable motive, or painful history.
- Current exile direction: the fourth city-state was blamed for a catastrophe that may have been caused by all four city-states together. Its exile became a cover-up, giving the remaining three city-states a clean public story and a scapegoat.
- The catastrophe was the Oathfire: the four city-states swore a sacred treaty under a divine or magical flame, but when the oath was broken, the flame burned through records, homes, and temples. The fourth city-state was blamed as the oathbreaker, though the truth is likely messier.
- Early symptoms can involve debts, contracts, trade pressure, disappearances, smugglers, altered records, and strange remnants of the erased city.

## Skill Affinities

- Skill affinities describe where skills are most common, not strict limits on who can learn or practice them.
- Food City-State: nature, nourishment, preparation, precision, mobility, and survival-adjacent skills.
- Repair City-State: material work, maintenance, equipment, durability, and direct physical combat skills.
- Knowledge-Magic City-State: study, refinement, magical power, healing, and formal spellcraft skills.
- Some skills may overlap between city-states. For example, alchemy belongs mostly to the Food City-State if treated as plant-based, kitchen-adjacent, herbal, and practical, but it could still have scholarly or magical branches elsewhere.

## Draft Reputation Factions

- Hearthcross: the central trading post and Fourth Little Power. Reputation comes from helping locals, protecting travelers, resolving disputes, improving the market, and supporting Mayor Elian Voss.
- Velmora: the Food City-State, Kitchen of the Road. Reputation comes from helping pilgrim kitchens, protecting harvest stores, working with herbs, alchemy, cooking, fishing, and defending guest-right.
- Brindlehook: the Repair City-State, City of Second Uses. Reputation comes from repairing damaged infrastructure, recovering lost tools, helping workshops, mining, crafting, blacksmithing, leatherworking, and fighting in direct melee styles.
- Aurelith: the Knowledge-Magic City-State, Gilded Academy. Reputation comes from recovering records, solving magical problems, assisting scholars, enchanting, restoration, and magic combat.
- Ashen Witnesses: hidden or unknown at first. This reputation should not openly reveal Solmere early. Early actions may secretly affect it through old contracts, missing records, northern symbols, debt disputes, and choices about whether to expose or suppress uncomfortable truths.

## Side Towns and Minor Factions

### Mossbank

- Type: smaller town within the Velmora city-state.
- Current concept: a fishing village or riverside settlement that adds depth to Velmora beyond farms, kitchens, and seed traditions.
- To flesh out later: local culture, notable NPCs, relationship to Velmora's old families, fishing-related quests, possible river or marsh mysteries.

### Lanternwrights

- Type: small organization that can appear in any city-state.
- Current concept: craftspeople who make and maintain road lanterns, market lamps, shrine lights, and signal lamps.
- To flesh out later: whether their lights reveal hidden marks, how they travel between city-states, what materials they need, and whether they know old road secrets.

### Tallyhouse

- Type: smaller organization based in Hearthcross.
- Current concept: a neutral accounting office or trade office that handles ordinary ledgers, invoices, contracts, market fees, and trade disputes.
- To flesh out later: staff, public trust, connection to Hearthcross politics, sidequests around counterfeit seals or missing invoices, and possible foreshadowing of Ashen Witness contract magic.

### Thimble Court

- Type: small faction associated with Aurelith's high-class society.
- Current concept: a formal circle of tailors, cobblers, leatherworkers, designers, and etiquette-minded judges of fashion and presentation.
- To flesh out later: social rules, noble patrons, crafting rewards, comedic disputes, and whether clothing carries magical or political meaning in Aurelith.

### Seedwardens

- Type: small but highly regarded organization in Velmora.
- Current concept: guardians of heirloom seeds, traveling herb bundles, crop lines, and possibly the seed vault.
- To flesh out later: ranks, rituals, connection to old families, seed vault duties, herbalism and alchemy quests, and possible involvement in a future dungeon.

### Quarry Families

- Type: smaller faction that adds depth to Brindlehook.
- Current concept: mining clans or stonecutting families who provide ore, stone, minerals, and raw materials for Brindlehook's repairs and crafts.
- To flesh out later: family rivalries, mine claims, relationship to Brindlehook workshops, mining quests, cave hazards, and possible old-road discoveries underground.

# Story draft:

The World
At the center of the region is Hearthcross, a cozy but politically important trading post where four old roads meet. Three roads are open: one to Velmora, the food city-state; one to Brindlehook, the repair city-state; and one to Aurelith, the knowledge-magic city-state. The fourth road leads north through Frostveil Pass, but it has been swallowed by unnatural snow, broken stone, and old fear.
Hearthcross has a shared market used by the three known city-states, but its homes and local life are independent. Its mayor, Elian Voss, is cheerful in public: good with travelers, quick with jokes, always making Hearthcross feel safe. In private, Elian is frightened. Strange old debts are appearing. Travelers are going missing. Ledgers mention a fourth city nobody is supposed to name.

The Four City-States
Velmora, the Kitchen of the Road, is warm, traditional, and ruled by old families. It feeds pilgrims and travelers as a sacred duty. It guards a seed vault full of ancient crop lines and old family promises.
Brindlehook, the City of Second Uses, believes everything can be repaired. Its people fix tools, armor, carts, locks, boots, heirlooms, and sometimes things that absolutely should have been thrown away. They are practical, funny, stubborn, and deeply proud of making broken things useful again.
Aurelith, the Gilded Academy, is refined and high-class. It preserves knowledge, magic, enchanting, and restoration. Its citizens are elegant, educated, and very aware of their own importance.
The lost fourth city was Solmere, the City of Witness. It kept records: treaties, debts, births, road-rights, verdicts, oaths, and trade laws. Solmere was not merely bureaucratic; it was sacred. To be recorded by Solmere meant the world itself had seen your promise.

The Oathfire
Long ago, all four city-states swore the Ember Compact at Hearthcross. The oath promised four things: no road would be closed, no traveler under guest-right would be harmed, no city would claim Hearthcross alone, and no shared record would be destroyed or altered.
The Compact was witnessed by the Oathfire, a sacred flame said to burn falsehood out of the world.
But over time, all four cities broke the oath. Velmora hid food during famine. Brindlehook built hidden lockworks to control road access. Aurelith altered magical records to protect noble families. Solmere gathered proof of these betrayals, but some within Solmere planned to use that proof to dominate the others.
Then the Oathfire erupted. It burned through homes, temples, archives, ledgers, and shrines. The northern road was devastated. Frostveil Pass froze over. Solmere was blamed as the oathbreaker, its people exiled, its name erased from maps, and its history rewritten.

The truth: everyone was guilty. Solmere simply became the scapegoat.

The Present Threat
Solmere’s descendants now move in secret as the Ashen Witnesses. Some want justice: recognition, restored names, reopened roads, and the truth of the cover-up revealed. Others want revenge. The most dangerous among them believe Solmere should return not as an equal city-state, but as the rightful ruler of the crossroads.
Early threats should look local: strange contracts, antique debt claims, missing travelers, smugglers, altered records, old seals, burned ledgers, and hidden Solmeri symbols. Only later does the pattern become clear.

Possible early dungeons:
a burned ledger cellar beneath Hearthcross
a Velmoran pilgrim kitchen where guest-right magic has turned dangerous
a Brindlehook repair yard where old locks rebuild themselves into forbidden road-gates
an Aurelith archive where erased Solmeri names reappear in gold ink
eventually, the frozen northern road through Frostveil Pass
